Titanium Knights

Team 15887 and 15891

The Titanium Knights is a robotics club located at the Bergen County Academies, a public magnet high school located in Hackensack, New Jersey. Our team is made of dedicated students with different levels of experience from four grade levels. For the 2023-24 season, we have two teams: All-Knighters 15887 and Knightmares 15891.

First Tech Challenge

FIRST Tech Challenge students learn to think like engineers. Teams design, build, and code robots to compete in an alliance format against other teams. Robots are built from a reusable platform, powered by Android technology, and can be coded using a variety of levels of Java-based programming.

CENTERSTAGE

This year, the Titanium Knights Robotics teams are competing in the 2023-2024 FTC Season, CENTERSTAGE. The game consists of an autonomous and driver-controlled period. Builders need to design and create a robot so that it can deposit pixels onto a board. Programmers need to use sensors to create an autonomous trajectory.

15887 & 15891

Our rookie season for FTC was the 2018-2019 FTC season, Rover Ruckus, after competing in FRC for numerous years. Composed of two sister teams, the Titanium Knights are a group of high school students interested in building, programming, documenting, and helping out our community.

How Our Team Works

Each team is split up into 3 different sections, build, programming, and outreach. Each section has their own responsibilities.

  • Build
    This team is responsible for actually building the robot, the build teams creates CAD models of the robot and takes the time to assemble the robot together and fix flaws that may exist with the build
  • Programming
    This team is responsible for programming the robot, once the robot is built it must be able to react from user movement. The programmers use Java and kotlin to make the robot interactive and controllable by a phone
  • Outreach
    Building robots is expensive, the non-tech is team is responsible for outreach with other companies to gain funding to build the robots, and attend the competitions. They are also responsible for everything else that is not related to build or programming.
